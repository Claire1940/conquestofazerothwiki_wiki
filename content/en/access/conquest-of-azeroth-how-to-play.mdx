<Section icon={Zap} title="Mastering the Talent System and Builds">

The talent system in Conquest of Azeroth is inspired by the modern Dragonflight-style trees. This means you have a **Class Tree** (general utility and core spells) and a **Specialization Tree** (role-specific power). Mastering these trees is essential for anyone looking to optimize their performance in both PvE and PvP.

<Callout type="success" title="Auto-Build Feature">

</Callout>

### Talent Progression Milestones

| Level | Milestone | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 3** | Class Quest | Unlocks your first major class-defining ability. |
| **Level 10** | Spec Choice | You must pick a primary specialization (e.g., Demolition for Tinker). |
| **Level 10+** | Point Allocation | Points alternate between the Class and Spec trees every level. |
| **Level 60** | Endgame Tuning | Access to the final tier of talents and capstone abilities. |

When creating a build, remember that synergy is key. For example, a **Pyromancer** focusing on the *Incineration* spec should prioritize talents that increase Ignite damage and critical strike chance to trigger faster meteors.

<Section icon={Target} title="Leveling Roadmap: From 1 to 60">

Leveling in Conquest of Azeroth is more than just grinding mobs; it is an exploration of a "Reborn Azeroth." The world has been updated with RPG creature stats, immersive loot drops, and hidden points of interest. Follow these steps to reach the level 60 cap efficiently.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Training (Levels 1-10)">
    Focus on your starting zone class trainer. At level 3, you will receive a prompt for your first class quest. Complete this immediately as it provides essential skills that define your early rotation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Specialization Unlock (Level 10)">
    Open your talent interface (default key 'N') and select your specialization. This will unlock your Spec Tree. If you are unsure, the "Valkyrie" path for Sun Clerics or "Animation" for Necromancers are very safe leveling choices.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Dungeon Integration (Levels 15-50)">
    Begin queueing for Normal Dungeons. These provide significant experience and class-specific gear. Pay attention to "Immersive Loot" drops in the open world, which often have unique stats tailored for custom classes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Endgame Preparation (Levels 50-60)">
    Transition into higher-level zones like Western Plaguelands or Silithus. Start looking for "Bloodforged" gear if you are playing in High Risk mode, as these items offer the best stats for entering level 60 raids like Molten Core.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Trainer Locations">
Class trainers are located in all major starting hubs, including Goldshire, Kharanos, and Brill. If you lose your way, guards in capital cities can now direct you to specific custom class trainers.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="PvE and PvP: The High Risk Economy">

Once you reach the endgame, the way you interact with other players depends on your chosen "Risk Mode." Conquest of Azeroth uses a phased system that allows different playstyles to coexist on the same realm.

### PvP Mode Comparison

| Mode | Risk Level | Ruleset | Rewards |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PvE Mode** | Low | Cannot be attacked in the open world. | Safe progression, Standard loot. |
| **PvP Mode** | Medium | Open-world combat enabled; no gear loss. | Honor points, World PvP titles. |
| **High Risk** | High | Dropping gear upon death; separate phase. | Bloodforged items, Enemy gear chests. |

<Callout type="warning" title="High Risk Warning">
In High Risk mode, defeating a player can result in them dropping a chest containing pieces of their equipped gear. Only enter this mode if you are comfortable with the "Full Loot" style of gameplay.
</Callout>
