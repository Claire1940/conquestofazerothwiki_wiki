<Callout type="success" title="Why S-Tier Rules">
S-tier healers are highly sought after because they make dungeon runs significantly faster. By converting their offensive rotations directly into smart-healing, they maintain party health while dealing competitive damage.

#### Cultist (Heretic / Influence)
The Cultist is the ultimate unholy paladin melee healer. Wearing heavy plate armor, they heal their group through shadow-infused melee strikes and void-touched spells. The core of their gameplay revolves around **Malevolence**, an ability that deals shadow damage while healing the five lowest-health allies within a massive 40-yard range for 70% of the damage dealt. 

Cultists also use **Abyssal Covenant** to link themselves to the main tank, transferring 10% of the tank's damage taken to themselves and converting 20% of all damage the Cultist deals into direct healing for the tank. By pooling Insanity to 100, the Cultist can transform into an Old God abomination every 45 seconds, unleashing massive bursts of damage and healing.

#### Pyromancer (Flameweaving)
The Pyromancer is a highly mobile, fire-wielding caster healer. Unlike traditional hard-casters, Pyromancers are encouraged to deal damage to pool **Embers**, which are then spent on high-impact healing spells when damage spikes occur. Their signature ability, **Cleansing Flames**, is a frontal cone AoE that heals allies while scorching enemies, making it incredibly powerful for clearing trash packs in dungeons.

Additionally, Pyromancers possess a legendary cheat-death mechanic: upon taking fatal damage, they transform into a **Phoenix**. During this form, they can continue casting spells uninterrupted, and once the duration ends, they revive with full health and mana, making them exceptionally forgiving in high-pressure encounters.

<Callout type="tip" title="A-Tier Gearing Tip">
A-tier healers often benefit heavily from hybrid gear stats. For example, Tinkers scale with both Agility and Intellect, while Bloodmages thrive on Intellect and Spirit to offset their health-spending costs.

#### Tinker (Invention)
The Tinker is a highly active, utility-dense healer that excels at battlefield control. They constantly place down **Healing Beacons** that shield and mend party members, which can be stacked to handle heavy damage spikes. Tinkers also summon turrets and robot pets that bring unique group buffs, and they can throw bombs or lay mines to assist the tank with crowd control. Their primary single-target tool is **Repair Shot**, which acts as a powerful smart-heal on the tank when properly augmented.

#### Venomancer (Vizier / Rot)
The Venomancer is the ultimate choice for players who love pure, restoration-focused gameplay. Heavily inspired by traditional Druids, Venomancers utilize specialized shapeshifting forms (such as spider form) and cover their allies in highly efficient healing-over-time (HoT) effects. While they are arguably the most fun and stable pure healers in the game, their main drawback is their low damage contribution, which keeps them just out of S-tier.

#### Bloodmage (Fleshweaver)
Bloodmages are ritualistic healers who spend their own health and build Rage to cast powerful spells. As a **Fleshweaver**, they spread blood rituals across the party, converting their own offensive damage into party healing. By building up to 10 stacks of **Cooled Vitality**, they unlock devastating AoE heals that drain the life essence of nearby enemies. This makes them exceptional raid healers, though their self-damaging nature requires careful resource management.

<Callout type="info" title="B-Tier Niche Play">
Do not write off B-tier healers entirely. In coordinated groups where positions are tightly controlled, a Primalist's melee-range heals can easily match S-tier output in raids.
</Callout>

#### Witch Doctor (Brewing / Voodoo)
The Witch Doctor has one of the most unique visual aesthetics in Conquest of Azeroth, featuring a bubbling cauldron carried on their back. They mix ingredients to maintain two active passive aura buffs for the party. At level 30, they unlock **Mojo Beam**, a highly engaging channeled heal that allows them to cast other spells simultaneously. 

However, outside of Mojo Beam, Witch Doctors often find themselves stuck in a repetitive loop of hard-casting single-target heals. The class is also heavily bloated with situational totems, hexes, and wards, which can overwhelm players with keybinds by the mid-20s.

#### Primalist (Life)
The Primalist is a sturdy, plate-wearing nature hybrid. They provide exceptional single-target healing and durable earth shields, making them phenomenal main-tank healers in raid environments. 

However, their biggest flaw is their strict range limitation. As a melee healer, the Primalist must remain close to their targets to heal effectively. If a dungeon party spreads out or a ranged DPS steps too far away, the Primalist struggles to reach them in time, often leading to avoidable deaths.

The C-tier on our **conquest of azeroth healer tier list** consists of classes that currently struggle with mana efficiency, rigid hard-casting requirements, or awkward resource loops. While highly thematic, they require significant effort for mediocre returns.

### C-Tier Class Comparison

| Class | Key Spec | Armor Type | Primary Stats | Main Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sun Cleric** | Piety / Blessings | Cloth / Plate | Strength, Intellect | Solar Power stacks, Dawn empowerment |
| **Starcaller** | Moon Priest | Mail / Plate | Intellect | Mana/Energy dual bar, Luna Eclipse, Scattered Stars |

<Callout type="warning" title="Mana Warning">
Both Sun Clerics and Starcallers suffer from severe mana management issues in early phases. Be prepared to carry plenty of water and communicate pull paces with your tank.
</Callout>

#### Sun Cleric (Piety / Blessings)
The Sun Cleric utilizes a blazing holy-fire theme, combining holy light with the raw power of fire. While they possess hard-hitting single-target heals and protective shields, their gameplay loop is incredibly slow. 

Sun Clerics must accumulate **Solar Power** stacks up to 30 to unleash **Dawn**, which empowers their next few spells. However, in practical group play, healers rarely have the luxury of holding on to this resource, forcing them to sit on a full bar while spamming slow, hard-casted **Illumination** spells from the backline.

#### Starcaller (Moon Priest)
The Starcaller is a complex healer that manages both a mana bar and an energy bar. Most of their heals cost a percentage of their maximum mana, and they must use energy-costing abilities to regenerate that mana back. They accumulate **Luna** stacks to trigger **Luna Eclipse** to make their next spells instant or AoE. 

While their single-target rotation is incredibly fun when no healing is required, they struggle heavily during large trash pulls. The sheer complexity of managing Luna phases, Scattered Stars debuffs on enemies, and dual resource bars makes them incredibly punishing for beginners.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="How to Optimize Your Healer Build">

Optimizing a healer in Conquest of Azeroth differs significantly from standard retail or classic WoW. Because many classes scale their heals off offensive damage, understanding your stat priorities and talent allocation is critical.

### Healer Stats & Armor Priority

| Role Style | Primary Stat | Secondary Stat | Preferred Armor | Key Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Melee Healer** | Strength / Intellect | Melee Hit / Haste | Plate / Mail | Boosts physical damage which scales passive heals |
| **Caster Healer** | Intellect | Spell Power / Crit | Cloth / Leather | Increases raw spell output and mana pool sizes |
| **Hybrid Utility** | Intellect / Spirit | Haste / Spell Hit | Mail / Leather | Faster cast times and improved resource regen |

<Callout type="tip" title="Hit Rating Importance">
If you are playing a damage-to-heal spec like the Cultist or Pyromancer, securing a small amount of Spell Hit or Melee Hit is vital. If your offensive spells miss the enemy target, your passive smart-heals will not trigger!
</Callout>

To successfully configure your character for group content, follow this step-by-step setup guide:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose Your Specialization at Level 10">
    Upon reaching level 10, open your talent interface and select your primary healing specialization (e.g., Heretic for Cultist, Flameweaving for Pyromancer). This unlocks your core healing passive and starting abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate Class and Spec Talents">
    Spend your talent points as you level up, alternating between the Class Tree and the Specialization Tree. If you are overwhelmed, use the built-in **Auto-Build** feature to allocate points safely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equip Role-Appropriate Gear">
    Prioritize gear that supports your primary scaling stats. Melee healers should look for hybrid Strength/Intellect pieces, while caster healers should focus purely on Intellect and Spell Power.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure Click-Heal Addons">
    Because several specs require targeting enemies while keeping an eye on party frames, installing a click-heal addon (like Clique or VuhDo) is highly recommended to streamline your reaction times.
  </Step>
</Steps>
