<Section icon={Target} title="A-Tier and B-Tier: Specialist Roles and Niche Picks">

While S-tier classes are universally strong, the A-tier and B-tier classes offer specialized tools that can dominate in specific scenarios, such as 3v3 Arenas or organized Battlegrounds.

**A-Tier: The Strong Contenders**
Classes like the **Starcaller** and **Sun Cleric** excel in group play. The Starcaller’s lunar barriers and the Sun Cleric’s holy fire provide a mix of offense and defense that is vital for securing objectives. **Tinkers** bring gadgets, turrets, and bombs that can turn a chokepoint into a death trap, making them the kings of Battleground defense.

**B-Tier: The Specialists**
These classes require more finesse or specific team compositions to shine. The **Chronomancer**, for instance, is a trickster mage that relies on time clones and rewinds. While their damage might not match a Pyromancer, their ability to displace enemies and reset fights is invaluable in the hands of a veteran.

<Section icon={Shield} title="PvP Modes: High Risk vs. Structured Combat">

Conquest of Azeroth offers several ways to engage in combat, each with its own risk profile. Your choice of class should reflect the mode you intend to play most frequently.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Risk Level">
    Choose between PvE Mode (safe), PvP Mode (open world combat), or High Risk PvP (separate phase where gear drops on death).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip Bloodforged Gear">
    In High Risk, you can find Bloodforged versions of dungeon items. These are more powerful but also highly coveted by enemy players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master Objective Play">
    In Battlegrounds and World Events like Crow's Cache, focus on the objective. A dead DPS does zero damage to a boss or flag carrier.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="High Risk Rewards">
