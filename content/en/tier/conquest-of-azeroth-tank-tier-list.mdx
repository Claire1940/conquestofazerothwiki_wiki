<Section icon={Swords} title="S-Tier: The Gold Standard for 2026">

The S-Tier represents the most robust and versatile tanks currently available in the Conquest of Azeroth ecosystem. These classes possess the perfect balance of physical mitigation, magical resistance, and threat generation.

**1. Guardian (Vanguard)**
The Guardian is the quintessential tank. Utilizing the **Vanguard** specialization, these warriors excel at traditional shield-based mitigation. They are the most stable choice for Mythic+ and Ascended raids because their damage intake is predictable. Their ability to plant Banners provides significant buffs to the entire party, making them a mandatory inclusion for progression guilds.

**2. Reaper (Domination)**
Reapers utilize the **Domination** tree to become shadow-infused juggernauts. Instead of relying purely on blocking, Reapers use **Soul Harvesting** and lifesteal mechanics to stay alive. They are arguably the best solo-leveling tanks because they rarely need to stop for food or water. In a group setting, their shadow tricks allow them to manipulate enemy positioning with ease.

**3. Knight of Xoroth (Defiance)**
If your goal is to manage massive packs of enemies, the Knight of Xoroth is unparalleled. The **Defiance** specialization uses **Deathfire** to power devastating AoE attacks. With their chains and hooks, they act as the ultimate battlefield controllers, pulling stray mobs back into the fray while burning them with hellfire.

| S-Tier Class | Best For | Survival Mechanic | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Guardian** | Raids/M+ | Block/Armor | Low |
| **Reaper** | Leveling/Solo | Lifesteal/Sustain | Medium |
| **Knight of Xoroth** | AoE/Dungeons | Hellfire Absorb | Medium |

<Callout type="success" title="Meta Recommendation">
For the July 2026 launch, we recommend the **Reaper** for players who plan to engage in High Risk PvP while leveling, as their self-sustain makes them incredibly difficult to gank.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Tanking Talent Systems and Builds">

The Dragonflight-style talent trees in Conquest of Azeroth allow for deep customization. Every tank must navigate two distinct trees: the **Class Tree** and the **Specialization Tree**.

At level 10, you will choose your primary specialization (e.g., Vanguard for Guardian). From that point forward, you will alternate talent points between the two trees. This prevents players from skipping essential utility in favor of raw power, ensuring every tank has the tools necessary to protect their team.

| Talent Type | Frequency | Purpose | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Class Point** | Every 2 levels | General Utility | Movement speed, Stamina |
| **Spec Point** | Every 2 levels | Role Mechanics | Shield Slam, Lifesteal |
| **Capstone** | End of Tree | Game-changing Power | AoE Taunt, Cheat Death |

**Auto-Build System:**
For new players overwhelmed by the 1000+ spells and talents, the **Auto-Build** option is a lifesaver. By selecting a "Tank" template, the game will automatically allocate your points into a viable build, allowing you to focus on learning the dungeon routes and threat mechanics.

<Callout type="info" title="Respec Info">
Respeccing is available at your class trainer. During the early Vol'Jin launch phase, keep a secondary DPS build ready for when you need to finish solo quests faster.
</Callout>

</Section>

  <Step num="2" title="The Level 3 Milestone">
