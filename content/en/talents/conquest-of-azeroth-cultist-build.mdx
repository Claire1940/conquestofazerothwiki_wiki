<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding the Cultist Class & Insanity Mechanic">

When choosing your path on Project Ascension's custom server, the **conquest of azeroth cultist build** offers one of the most thematic and versatile playstyles in the Classic+ ecosystem. As a servant of the Old Gods, the Cultist balances on a razor's edge, utilizing mana and a unique resource called Insanity. While other classes fear the void, the Cultist harnesses it to heal allies, absorb incoming strikes, or transform into terrifying Eldritch entities. However, pushing your mind too far has dire consequences, making resource management the absolute core of this class.

The defining mechanic of the Cultist is Insanity. Every time you cast a shadow spell or land a dark melee strike, your Insanity bar fills up. For DPS and healing specs, reaching 100 Insanity results in temporary madness, causing your character to run around randomly casting spells while losing a massive portion of health. This makes pacing and spell selection critical. Conversely, for the Dreadnought tank spec, reaching 100 Insanity is the ultimate goal, triggering a monstrous transformation that dramatically increases your durability and magic resistance.

<Callout type="info" title="Insanity Warning">
Insanity decays slowly over time when out of combat, allowing you to reset your resources between pulls. Always keep an eye on your UI to avoid accidental madness during high-intensity dungeon encounters.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Conquest of Azeroth Cultist Build: The Godblade Melee Guide">

The Godblade is a devastating physical-shadow hybrid spec that channels the power of the Old Gods directly into a two-handed weapon. This **conquest of azeroth cultist build** is perfect for players who love aggressive, high-risk melee combat. By combining physical weapon damage with shadow-infused strikes, Godblades control the battlefield with void tendrils and psychic suppression while dancing dangerously close to the brink of insanity.

Your core rotation revolves around generating Void Runes (similar to rogue combo points) using Eldritch Strike, and spending them on Blade of the Empire. Thanks to the Eldritch Bargain passive, 35% of your Strength is converted into Shadow Spell Power, and 15% is converted into Spell Hit. This unique scaling allows you to stack Strength to boost both your melee strikes and your shadow spells, making you a true hybrid powerhouse.

| Stat | Priority | Ideal Gear Type | Key Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Primary (Highest) | Plate / Mail (Str) | Converts to shadow spell power and hit rating |
| **Stamina** | Secondary | Heavy armor with Stam | Boosts survivability and scales shadow absorption |
| **Haste** | Tertiary | Attack Speed / Haste | Speeds up melee strikes and Void Rune generation |
| **Intellect** | Low | Hybrid Strength/Int | Useful for mana pool but secondary to Strength |

| Spell Name | Resource Cost | Cooldown | Combat Role & Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Eldritch Strike** | Mana | 3 seconds | Generates 1 Void Rune; deals 90% weapon damage as Shadow |
| **Blade of the Empire** | 1+ Void Runes | None | Spends runes to deal heavy Shadow damage; generates Insanity |
| **Reeving Blade** | Mana | 8 seconds | AoE swing dealing 120% weapon damage to nearby enemies |
| **Nether Strike** | Mana | 20 seconds | Heavy strike; reduces enemy shield duration by 60% |

<Callout type="success" title="Synergy Bonus">
By using Reeving Blade first, your next Eldritch Strike on any hit target deals 30% more damage. Always use this combo for massive AoE clears in dungeons!
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="The Dreadnought Tank Build: Transforming into Eldritch Horror">

The Dreadnought is one of the most unique tank builds in Conquest of Azeroth. While other specs must carefully manage their Insanity to avoid losing control, the Dreadnought actively embraces the madness. Your primary goal is to generate Insanity as quickly as possible to reach 100 stacks. Upon hitting maximum Insanity, you do not go mad; instead, you transform into a hulking, corrupted Eldritch horror, drastically boosting your armor, health, and magic resistance.

This build is exceptionally strong against magic-heavy encounters, making you the ultimate spellcaster counter. By utilizing a shield and a one-handed weapon, you combine traditional tanking mechanics with powerful shadow spells to maintain high threat on multiple targets.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build Insanity Quickly">
    Use abilities like Armageddon and Presence of Y'Shaarj to pull mobs and rapidly generate Insanity stacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Trigger Eldritch Ascension">
    Once you hit 100 Insanity, ascend into your corrupted Dreadnought form to unlock massive defensive bonuses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Mitigate Damage and Hold Aggro">
    Maintain your active mitigation by keeping Twilight Shield active and using shadow-based threat generation spells.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage the Decay Phase">
    As your Insanity decays, prepare your next rotation of high-generation spells to quickly re-ascend into horror form.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Spell Name | Primary Effect | Cooldown | Best Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Armageddon** | High AoE shadow damage and heavy threat | 15 seconds | Opening pulls to secure threat on packs |
| **Presence of Y'Shaarj** | Massive threat multiplier and shadow damage | None | Active aura; keep toggled on during combat |
| **Twilight Shield** | Absorbs incoming physical and magical damage | 12 seconds | Active mitigation; use before heavy boss hits |
| **Abyssal Ward** | Protects against up to 3 incoming attacks | 30 seconds | Emergency defensive when taking heavy physical damage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Tank Mitigation Warning">
While in Eldritch horror form, your magic resistance is unmatched, but you are still vulnerable to heavy physical armor-piercing strikes. Coordinate with your healer during physical-heavy boss phases.
</Callout>

</Section>
