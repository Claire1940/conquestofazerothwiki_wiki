<Section icon={Zap} title="Talent System and Build Customization">

In the **conquest of azeroth full release**, the talent system has been completely overhauled to a dual-tree structure. At level 10, players unlock their primary specialization. From there, talent points alternate between a **Class Tree** (utility and core tools) and a **Specialization Tree** (spec-specific power).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Specialization">
    Reach level 10 and open the talent interface to choose one of your class's 3+ specializations. This decision defines your primary role (e.g., choosing *Demolition* for a Tinker).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate Talent Points">
    As you level up, you receive points for both the Class Tree and the Spec Tree. The Class Tree usually contains defensive and mobility tools, while the Spec Tree enhances your core rotation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enable Auto-Build">
    If you are overwhelmed by the 1,000+ available spells and talents, use the **Auto-Build** feature. It automatically allocates points into a proven setup so you can focus on combat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Refine and Respec">
    Once you understand your class loop, visit a class trainer to respec. This allows you to fine-tune your build for specific content like PvP or Raiding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Build Flexibility">
The "Dragonflight-style" trees allow for significant experimentation. Most hybrid classes like the **Sun Cleric** or **Starcaller** can switch between Tank and Healer roles simply by swapping their specialization and adjusting a few talent points.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="PvP and the High Risk System">

PvP in the **conquest of azeroth full release** is divided into three distinct modes to cater to different player preferences. Whether you want safe structured combat or the adrenaline of losing gear, CoA has a system in place.

- **PvE Mode**: Safe exploration. You cannot be targeted by players unless you flag yourself.
- **PvP Mode**: Standard open-world combat. No gear loss, but full conflict is enabled.
- **High Risk PvP**: A separate phase where players drop chests containing their gear upon death. This mode rewards players with "Bloodforged" items found in the open world.

| PvP Mode | Risk Level | Reward Type | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Battlegrounds** | Structured | PvP Currency / Rank | Team Objective Players |
| **Arenas (1v1-3v3)** | Competitive | Rating / Titles | Skill-Based Duelists |
| **High Risk Phase** | **Extreme** | Enemy Gear Drops | Hardcore Loot Hunters |
| **World Events** | Variable | Crow's Cache / Blood Bowl | Guilds and Solo Killers |

<Callout type="warning" title="High Risk Warning">
High Risk is a separate phase. If you enter this mode, you accept that your equipped items can be lost to other players. Use this mode only when you are confident in your build and have backup gear sets.
</Callout>
