<Section icon={Swords} title="Blood Mage Spell Families and Roles">

Blood Mage is the cleanest example of how Conquest of Azeroth spells can merge damage, healing, and survivability into one kit. The class channels its own life force, builds rage, and can bend that power into different forms. The result is a caster that can fight like a monster, sustain like a drain tank, or support a group with blood rituals.

| Specialization | Core Loop | Role | Best Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flesh Weaver | Build pooled vitality, then convert it into healing | Healer | Group content and sustained recovery |
| Sanguin | Spend your own life to amplify dark spells | DPS | Burst damage and aggressive play |
| Cursed | Cast as a mage, then transform into a vampiric fighter | DPS | Close-range swaps and hybrid pressure |
| Lycanthropy | Fully shed mortality and fight as a savage warg | Tank | Frontline control and damage soaking |

The Blood Mage highlight is flexibility. You can sink into pools to avoid damage, drain enemies to recover, or transform into monstrous forms that change your combat range. That means the class rewards players who can read the fight and switch modes at the right moment.

| Blood Mage Spell | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| Blood Pools | Deny space and enable safe repositioning | AoE fights and kiting |
| Vampiric Fang | Drains life from a target | Sustain and clutch recovery |
| Life Drinker | Converts enemy health into your own | Extended fights |
| Dark Rituals | Empower allies or summon pressure | Group utility |
| Werewolf Summons | Adds extra bodies to the fight | Tanking and chaos |
