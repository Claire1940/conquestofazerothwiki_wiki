The **conquest of azeroth forum** and related community hubs serve as the central meeting grounds for players exploring Project Ascension's highly ambitious custom WoW server. Unlike standard custom realms that tweak existing talent trees, Conquest of Azeroth (CoA) introduces a complete re-imagining of the Vanilla world. 

Forum discussions highlight the sheer scale of the project, which replaces the original 9 classes with 21 custom-built classes and 69 specs forged from Warcraft lore. While early alpha and beta feedback praised the class fantasy, community threads focus on balancing, gameplay pacing, and how the game integrates with Project Ascension's existing ecosystem.

### Popular Classes & Mechanics

*   **Necromancer**: A cloth-wearing DPS that utilizes **Life Force** to summon hordes of undead pets, spread plagues, or transform into a powerful Lich.
*   **Cultist**: A versatile class (Tank/DPS/Healer) that generates **Insanity**. Players must carefully balance their insanity bar; letting it cap out results in instant death.
*   **Tinker**: Uses Agility or Intellect to execute explosive demolition rotations, deploy mechanical turrets, or pilot a Shredder mechsuit.
*   **Chronomancer**: A temporal trickster capable of manipulating time to heal allies, create time-stuck clones, or unleash dual-natured order and chaos magic.
*   **Witch Hunter**: An agile monster hunter utilizing dual crossbows, muskets, or fiery blades to purge targets.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Server Features & Hybrid Risk System">

Forum users frequently discuss the server's approach to player split, which is solved via the **Hybrid Risk System**. This system allows players of completely different playstyles to occupy the same world phase without dividing the community.

| Phase Mode | PvP Status | Unique Rewards & Rules |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PvE Mode** | Completely untargetable | Standard questing, dungeons, and raids |
| **PvP Mode** | Open-world PvP enabled | Standard server rules, shared PvE phase |
| **High Risk PvP** | Full loot drop on death | Drops random equipped/inventory items to victor |

### Gearing in High Risk Mode
While in High Risk PvP, players gain the **Treasure Hunter** buff. This allows monsters in the open world to drop **Bloodforged** versions of Dungeon and Raid gear. This equipment is non-soulbound, meaning players can freely sell, trade, or buy high-tier gear on the Auction House.

<Callout type="info" title="High Risk Tip">
If you enjoy open-world progression without running structured instances, leveling in High Risk PvP lets you obtain rare dungeon items directly from world monsters.
</Callout>
