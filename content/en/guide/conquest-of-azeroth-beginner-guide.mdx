<Section icon={Target} title="The Talent System and Progression">

The progression system in Conquest of Azeroth is inspired by modern talent designs. Starting at level 10, players unlock two distinct trees: the **Class Tree** and the **Specialization Tree**. As you level up, you will alternate between receiving points for these trees, ensuring a balanced growth of utility and raw power.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Reach Level 3">
    Watch for the on-screen prompt to find your **Class Trainer**. This is located in the starting hubs like Goldshire or Brill.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Unlock Specialization">
    At **Level 10**, open the talent interface (N) and choose your primary specialization. This choice dictates your core combat role.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Allocate Talent Points">
    Spend points in the Class Tree for general utility and the Spec Tree for role-specific power. Use **Auto-Build** if you want a guided path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Visit Trainers Regularly">
    Unlike retail WoW, you must visit trainers to learn new ranks of spells or specifically chosen abilities that aren't auto-learned.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Level Bracket | Major Milestone | Unlockable Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 - 10** | Basic Rotation | Class Quest & Trainer Access |
| **10 - 30** | Role Definition | Spec Choice & First Talent Points |
| **30 - 50** | Mid-Game Mastery | Advanced Class Mechanics (e.g., Mechs) |
| **50 - 60** | Endgame Prep | Dungeon Heroics & Initial Raiding |

<Callout type="success" title="Respec Flexibility">
You can reset your talents at your Class Trainer. This is highly recommended if you find your current build lacks survivability in higher-level zones.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Endgame: PvE and PvP Modes">

Once you reach the level cap, the **conquest of azeroth beginner guide** transitions into endgame systems. The realm offers a hybrid risk system, allowing players to choose their level of engagement with open-world conflict.

| Mode Type | Risk Level | Core Mechanics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PvE Mode** | **Low** | Untargetable in open world; focus on Dungeons/Raids. |
| **PvP Mode** | **Medium** | Open-world combat enabled; no gear loss. |
| **High Risk** | **High** | Separate phase; players drop gear chests upon death. |
| **Arenas** | **Competitive** | 1v1, 2v2, and 3v3 ranked matches. |

### Dungeons and Raids
The PvE endgame includes classic Vanilla dungeons and raids, but with entirely new tuning for the custom classes. You can progress through Normal, Heroic, Mythic, and the challenging **Mythic+** system for 5-man content. Raids like **Molten Core** and **Onyxia** feature "Ascended" difficulties for top-tier rewards.

<Callout type="warning" title="High Risk Warning">
Entering the **High Risk PvP** phase is a separate world instance. Only enter if you are prepared to lose equipped items to other players.
</Callout>
