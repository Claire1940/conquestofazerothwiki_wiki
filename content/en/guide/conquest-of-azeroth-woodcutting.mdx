<Section icon={Coins} title="Turning Woodcutting Into Gold">

Woodcutting only feels weak when you treat logs as the final product. In practice, the value comes from what those materials unlock: crafting XP, stronger gear, and a steady flow of sellable resources. Ascension-style crafting can roll extra affixes and receive upgrades, so a gathering profession can support a much larger gearing plan.

<Callout type="success" title="Profit Rule">
If a material helps crafting, upgrades, or a reliable sale on the market, it deserves a spot in your inventory plan.
</Callout>

| Item Flow | Best Action | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Raw wood | Sell or bank early | Stable gold and less bag clutter |
| Extra mats | Save for crafting | More XP and better gear access |
| Upgrade-ready gear | Keep and improve | Stronger item stats over time |
| Low-value clutter | Vendor immediately | Fewer route breaks |

Many players also forget that exploration matters. World Forged items can appear in places that used to be empty, and those side discoveries can be worth more than a single extra node. If your route crosses a sparkling object, check it before you rush past. The smartest gatherers combine tree loops with opportunistic pickups.

| Priority | What To Watch For | Why It Pays Off |
|---|---|---|
| Tree density | Clusters near roads and hubs | Better uptime |
| Inventory space | Empty bags before each run | Longer gathering sessions |
| Crafting use | Match wood to a recipe | Turning mats into progress |
| World pickups | Sparkly objects and hidden loot | Extra value without major detours |
