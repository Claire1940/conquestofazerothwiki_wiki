<Section icon={Shield} title="PvP Modes and Risk Management">

When leveling, you can toggle your PvP preference through the Honor tab (H key) in capital cities [1]. This choice dictates how you interact with other players in the open world and carries varying levels of risk and reward [1].

*   **Safe Haven**: Cross-faction safety. You cannot be attacked by other players under any circumstances [1].
*   **No Risk PvP**: Standard PvP flagging. You can engage in skirmishes with no penalties or loot loss upon death [1].
*   **High Risk PvP**: High-stakes combat. Dying to another player causes you to drop items into a lootable chest [1].

Because world scaling is active, high-risk PvP can occasionally pit you against players of significantly higher levels who are questing in the same low-level zones [1]. To combat griefing, a level-difference restriction prevents players from attacking you if they are more than five levels higher [1].

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
If you do not enjoy open-world PvP or fear losing your hard-earned gear, **do not select High Risk PvP** [1]. Stick to Safe Haven for a peaceful leveling experience [1].
</Callout>

</Section>
