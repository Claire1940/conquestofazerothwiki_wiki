<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Leveling & Talent Progression">

Leveling a **conquest of azeroth starcaller** requires a balance between talent point allocation and skill acquisition. Because the custom Dragonflight-style talent trees alternate points between the Class Tree and the Specialization Tree, planning your route ensures a smooth journey from level 1 to the level 60 cap.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Early Lunar Spells (Levels 1-10)">
    Begin your journey in starting zones like Goldshire or Brill. Focus on using your baseline ranged lunar spells to pull enemies from a distance. Visit your class trainer at level 3 to complete your initial class quest, which unlocks your core Lunar Power generation mechanic.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Specialization (Level 10)">
    Upon reaching level 10, open your talent interface and select your primary specialization. For fast and safe solo leveling, Sentinel (Ranged DPS) or Moon Guard (Melee DPS) are highly recommended due to their high self-sustain and quick kill times.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Talent Point Allocation (Levels 10-45)">
    As you level up, alternate your talent points between utility nodes in the Starcaller Class Tree and damage/healing boosters in your Specialization Tree. If you find the talent system overwhelming, use the built-in Auto-Build feature to automatically distribute points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Endgame Transition (Levels 45-60)">
    Refine your spell rotation and start replacing generic quest gear with spec-specific armor. Prepare for level 60 content by testing your build in high-risk zones, normal dungeons, and early raid phases like Molten Core.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch Your Class Trainer">
Do not skip the level 3 trainer quest. Early pacing is heavily reliant on finding your trainer in starting zones to unlock your core rotational abilities. Without these, your leveling speed will drop significantly.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Starcaller Gear & Stat Priority">

Gearing your Starcaller correctly is the difference between struggling against elite monsters and breezing through mythic dungeons. Because Starcallers utilize Mail and Plate armor, they are naturally sturdier than cloth-wearing spellcasters. However, you must prioritize stats that complement your chosen specialization.

While Intellect remains the absolute foundation for all Starcaller builds, secondary stats dictate your efficiency in combat. Wardens require defensive avoidance, Sentinels crave critical strikes, Moon Priests rely on mana regeneration, and Moon Guards benefit from attack speed.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Endgame PvE & PvP Strategies">

At level 60, the Starcaller transitions into a formidable force in both coordinated raids and chaotic battlegrounds. In PvP, the Starcaller relies on its hybrid nature to outlast opponents. Sentinel players can easily kite melee classes using lunar slows and displacement spells, while Moon Guards can burst down healers with high physical-astral damage combos.
