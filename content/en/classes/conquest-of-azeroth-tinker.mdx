<Section icon={Wrench} title="Class Overview and Core Mechanics">

The **conquest of azeroth tinker** stands out as one of the 21 original classes introduced in the Project Ascension ecosystem. Unlike traditional classes that rely on mana or rage, the Tinker is a master of "Rounds" and mechanical ingenuity. This class is designed for players who enjoy a high-utility playstyle, blending direct damage with automated systems like turrets and beacons.

In the Reborn Azeroth environment, Tinkers wear Leather or Mail armor, giving them a significant durability advantage over cloth-wearing casters. Their primary stats are split between Agility and Intellect, allowing for diverse build paths that can pivot between raw explosive power and technical support.

| Feature | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Roles** | **Damage Dealer (DPS), Healer** |
| **Armor Type** | **Leather, Mail** |
| **Primary Stats** | **Agility, Intellect** |
| **Core Resource** | **Rounds** |
| **Weaponry** | **Crossbows, Muskets, One-Handed Maces, Shields** |
| **Unique Mechanic** | **Automated Turrets & Nanobots** |

The "Rounds" mechanic functions as a tactical resource. Certain abilities generate Rounds, while your most powerful gadgets—like rocket launchers or high-tier turrets—consume them. Mastering the flow of Round generation is the difference between a novice engineer and a master Tinker.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Leveling Guide">

Leveling a **conquest of azeroth tinker** requires a balance of questing and understanding your class trainer's location. Since CoA uses a custom world structure, your early progression is vital for unlocking core gadgets.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Training (Levels 1-10)">
    Start in your racial zone and immediately look for the Level 3 class quest prompt. Find your class trainer in Goldshire, Kharanos, or Brill to unlock your first Round-generating ability.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spec Selection (Level 10)">
    Unlock your primary specialization. For the fastest leveling, select **Mechanics**. This allows you to deploy turrets that tank mobs while you fire from a distance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Core Rotation (Levels 11-40)">
    Focus on talents that increase Round generation and turret duration. Replace your gear frequently, prioritizing Intellect if you use turrets (as they scale with spell power) or Agility for direct shots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Advanced Gadgetry (Levels 40-60)">
    Unlock your specialization's signature move—such as the Shredder Mechsuit for Mechanics or the Mega-Bomb for Demolition. Visit your trainer every 2 levels to ensure your gadgets are up to date.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Gearing and Stat Priority">

Gearing your **conquest of azeroth tinker** depends heavily on your chosen role. Because Tinkers can wear Mail at higher levels, they are significantly sturdier than Mages or Priests. However, choosing the wrong stats can render your gadgets ineffective.

| Priority | Stat | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary** | **Intellect** | Increases spell power for turrets, nanobots, and explosive damage. |
| **Secondary** | **Agility** | Boosts critical strike chance and physical damage for muskets/crossbows. |
| **Tertiary** | **Stamina** | Vital for surviving High Risk PvP or world boss encounters. |
| **Utility** | **Haste** | Reduces the global cooldown and speeds up turret deployment. |

When selecting gear, look for "of the Elder" or "of the Eagle" suffixes early on. These provide the best mix of Intellect and Stamina. As you approach the level 60 endgame on the Vol'Jin realm, you will want to hunt for class-specific gear drops in dungeons like Blackrock Depths.

<Callout type="success" title="Gear Strategy">
In the 2026 meta, "Bloodforged" versions of gear from High Risk PvP phases are highly sought after by Tinkers for their bonus mechanical scaling properties.
</Callout>
