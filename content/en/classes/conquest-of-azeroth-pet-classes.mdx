<Section icon={Swords} title="Necromancer Minion Mechanics and Builds">

To maximize your minion efficiency, you must understand the specific roles of each undead summon. Some minions act as front-line shields, while others provide ranged spell support or utility buffs.

| Minion Type | Primary Role | Key Ability | Best Scenario |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Ranger and Tinker Pet Synergy">

For players who prefer mechanical precision or wild beasts over the undead, the Ranger and Tinker offer highly competitive alternatives. The Ranger focuses on a singular, powerful beast companion that benefits from your ranged physical attacks. This creates a highly dynamic playstyle where you kite enemies while your pet attacks from behind.

Tinkers, on the other hand, utilize stationary turrets and mobile mechanical constructs. This class relies heavily on zone control, making it incredibly strong in defensive PvP scenarios and structured raid encounters.

| Pet / Construct | Buff Provided | Duration | Cooldown |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wolf (Ranger)** | Furious Howl (AP Buff) | 20 seconds | 40 seconds |
| **Flame Turret (Tinker)** | Fire Vulnerability | Permanent (Active) | None |
| **Shield Generator (Tinker)** | Damage Absorption | 15 seconds | 60 seconds |

<Callout type="success" title="Tinker Placement Strategy">
Positioning your Flame Turrets near corners or choke points ensures enemies take maximum damage before they can reach your character.
</Callout>

Rangers should focus on Agility and Physical Hit Rating to ensure both their own shots and their pet's attacks land reliably. Tinkers must prioritize Haste, as it directly increases the attack speed of all deployed turrets, leading to a substantial boost in sustained damage output.

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Optimize Pet Class Progression">

Optimizing your character progression is essential if you want to tackle high-level content. Gearing a pet-based character requires a different approach than gearing a standard spellcaster or melee fighter. Follow these structured steps to ensure your character is optimized for the end-game meta.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Gear Selection">
    Focus entirely on acquiring gear with your primary stat (Intellect for Necromancers and Tinkers, Agility for Rangers). Avoid complex secondary stats early on.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Hit Cap Optimization">
    Ensure you reach the physical or spell hit cap for your current level. If your character misses an attack, your pet's scaling efficiency drops dramatically.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unlock Class Talents">
    Allocate your talent points into nodes that directly increase pet health, damage inheritance, and attack speed before boosting your own direct damage spells.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Acquire Custom Glyphs">
    Equip custom class glyphs that modify pet behaviors, such as reducing cooldowns on pet utility spells or increasing their resistance to area-of-effect damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Gearing Advice">
Do not neglect your own survivability. A dead summoner cannot command their pets, so ensure you maintain a reasonable pool of Stamina alongside your offensive stats.
</Callout>
