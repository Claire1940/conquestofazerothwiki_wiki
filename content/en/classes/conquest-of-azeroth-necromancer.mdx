<Section icon={Map} title="Conquest of Azeroth Necromancer Class Overview">

The **conquest of azeroth necromancer** stands out as one of the most popular and powerful custom classes in Project Ascension's custom WoW setting. Operating as a pure cloth-wearing ranged DPS caster, this class captures the dark fantasy of commanding the dead and decaying enemies with shadow and frost magic. Unlike standard WoW classes, the Necromancer relies on a unique resource system called Life Force, which is harvested from combat and spent to fuel powerful spells or maintain hordes of undead minions.

When playing a Necromancer, you are not just a shadow-themed mage or a warlock with different pet skins. The class features entirely custom mechanics, visual effects, and a dual-tree talent system inspired by modern expansion designs. Whether you prefer melting single targets with spreading plagues, swarming bosses with a pocket army of skeletons, or locking down battlegrounds with cold decay, the Necromancer offers highly versatile playstyles across its three specializations.

<Section icon={Target} title="Optimal Talent Trees & Step-by-Step Leveling Progression">

Leveling your **conquest of azeroth necromancer** requires navigating the custom Dragonflight-style talent system. Starting at level 10, you will alternate between spending points in the general Necromancer Class Tree and your chosen Specialization Tree. The general Class Tree provides utility, defensive barriers, and passive resource generation, while your Specialization Tree unlocks active combat spells and mechanical multipliers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Basics (Levels 1-10)">
    Begin your journey in your starting zone. Focus on learning your basic shadow bolt and life-draining spells. Keep an eye out for your level 3 class quest prompt, which guides you to your local Necromancer trainer to unlock your first minion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Spec & Activate Auto-Build (Level 10)">
    Upon reaching level 10, open your talent interface and lock in your primary specialization. If you are new to custom WoW mechanics, use the built-in **Auto-Build** option. This system automatically allocates your talent points into a balanced, pre-tested build as you level up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Your Core Rotation (Levels 10-35)">
    Spend your talent points to unlock core rotation spells. If playing Animation, prioritize skeletal archers. If playing Death, focus on talent nodes that increase plague duration and allow your DoTs to tick faster. Ensure you keep your gear updated with Intellect stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Refine Utility and Defenses (Levels 35-55)">
    Use this bracket to path into the general Class Tree for defensive tools. Unlock active crowd control, spell interrupts, and life-siphoning shields. These tools are crucial for surviving dungeon mechanics and handling sudden open-world PvP encounters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Transition to Endgame (Levels 55-60)">
    As you approach the level 60 cap on the Vol'Jin realm, reset your talents to transition from a leveling setup to a dedicated PvE or PvP build. Dungeon builds require more area-of-effect (AoE) damage and utility, whereas leveling builds focus on single-target efficiency.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Level Bracket | Target Goals | Key Unlocks | Trainer Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 - 10** | Complete starting zone; reach level 10 | Basic spells, first pet | Yes, visit trainer at level 3 and 10 |
| **10 - 30** | Unlock specialization; build core rotation | Specialization Tree, Auto-Build | No, talents can be spent anywhere |
| **30 - 50** | Run dungeons; optimize gear | Advanced pets, plague multipliers | Yes, check for updated spell ranks |
| **50 - 60** | Prepare for endgame; farm pre-BiS | Capstone talents, ultimate spells | Yes, finalize all active custom abilities |

<Callout type="warning" title="Talent Reset Warning">
Resetting your talent trees is highly accessible, but doing so inside active dungeons or battlegrounds is restricted. Always visit a safe sanctuary or major city before attempting to re-spec your Necromancer.
</Callout>
