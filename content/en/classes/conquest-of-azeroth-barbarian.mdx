<Section icon={Target} title="Brutality Specialization: Melee Destruction">

The Brutality spec represents the pinnacle of close-quarters combat. Players choosing this path must decide between two distinct weapon configurations: the massive Two-Hander or the relentless Dual-Wield setup. Each path offers a unique rhythm to combat, focusing on armor penetration and finishing moves.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Two-Handed Path">

    - **Decapitate**: Scoring a kill resets this powerful skill.

  </Card>
  <Card title="Dual-Wield Path">
    - **Whirlwind of Steel**: Rapid, armor-piercing strikes.

  </Card>

One of the most unique mechanics in the Brutality tree is the **Death Match**. This ability forces a 1v1 scenario where both the Barbarian and their target have their armor reduced to zero. It is a literal race to see who can tear the other to shreds first, perfectly encapsulating the savage nature of the class.

<Callout type="warning" title="Death Match Warning">
Only use Death Match when you are confident in your burst potential or have your low-HP healing buffs active, as you will be just as vulnerable as your enemy.
</Callout>

| **Death Match** | Reduces armor to 0 for self and target | 2m |
| **Brutal Strikes** | Increases armor penetration by 15% | Passive |
| **Cleave** | Hits up to 3 targets for 70% damage | None |

<Section icon={Map} title="Head Hunting: Ranged Precision & Tribal Mastery">

Inspired by the ancient techniques of the Trolls, the Head Hunting specialization transforms the **Conquest of Azeroth Barbarian** into a deadly ranged predator. Unlike traditional hunters who rely on pets, the Head Hunter relies on their own strength, hurling throwing axes and spears with lethal accuracy.

This spec focuses heavily on kiting and mobility. By utilizing **Berserker Rush**, the Barbarian can quickly disengage from melee range, creating the space needed to rain down a storm of projectiles. The goal is to keep enemies at a distance while they bleed out from multiple puncture wounds.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Bleeds">
    Open with Throwing Spears to apply deep wounds and slow the target's movement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Maintain Distance">
    Use Berserker Rush or movement speed buffs to stay outside the enemy's melee range.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute">
    Once the target is weakened by bleeds, use high-velocity axe throws to finish them off.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Mobility is Key">
Head Hunters gain increased movement speed naturally. Use this to your advantage to outmaneuver opponents in both PvP and PvE scenarios.
</Callout>

| Ranged Tool | Primary Function | Status Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Throwing Axe** | Fast, consistent damage filler | Minor Bleed |
| **Heavy Spear** | High damage, long-range opener | Impale (Slow) |
| **Berserker Rush** | Quick repositioning tool | Speed Boost |
| **Barbed Hook** | Pulls enemies or keeps them in place | Snare |

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Ancestry: The Frost-Touched Vrykul Path">

The Ancestry spec is perhaps the most unique of the three, blending melee combat with frost-based magic and powerful group utility. Drawing upon their Vrykul lineage, these Barbarians use an ancient resource known as the **Tanker**. This tanker fills over time, and "drinking" from it provides significant tactical advantages.

Ancestry Barbarians are highly valued in group content for their supportive capabilities. They don't just deal damage; they enhance the entire party's effectiveness through various blessings and resistances.
