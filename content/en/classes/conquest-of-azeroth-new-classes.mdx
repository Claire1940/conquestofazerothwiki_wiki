<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Talent Trees and Specialization Mechanics">

The talent system in Conquest of Azeroth is one of its most sophisticated features, drawing heavy inspiration from modern "Dragonflight" structures. At level 10, players must make their most important choice: their primary specialization. This choice unlocks one of the 69 available specs across the 21 classes and dictates the spells you will prioritize at class trainers.

**How the Talent System Works:**
- **Dual Trees**: You have access to a **Class Tree** (utility and core identity) and a **Specialization Tree** (role-specific power).
- **Point Allocation**: Talent points alternate between the two trees as you level up, preventing you from ignoring utility for pure power.
- **Auto-Build Support**: A built-in system can automatically allocate points based on community-vetted builds, which is perfect for your first run to level 60.

| Spec Example | Class | Focus | Key Mechanic |

| **Flameweaving** | Pyromancer | Fire-based Healing | Phoenix Rebirth |
| **Godblade** | Cultist | Shadow Melee DPS | Insanity Spend |
| **Warden** | Starcaller | Lunar Tanking | Astral Barriers |

<Callout type="info" title="Respecing">
Don't fear experimentation. The system allows for easy respecing, enabling you to test different specializations within your class as you find new gear or transition from solo leveling to group dungeons.
</Callout>

The **Conquest of Azeroth new classes** are further defined by the "Auto-Build" paths. For instance, a **Venomancer** can choose the "Rot" path for damage-over-time pressure or the "Fortitude" path for poison-infused tanking. These paths ensure that even complex classes remain accessible to the average player while maintaining a high skill ceiling for veterans.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Leveling Guide">

Leveling from 1 to 60 in Conquest of Azeroth requires a different mindset than standard Vanilla WoW. The world has been "reborn" with RPG creature stats and immersive loot drops that specifically support the custom class archetypes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Account and Launcher Setup">
    Create an Ascension account and download the official launcher. Ensure you select the **Conquest of Azeroth** realm path (Vol'Jin) rather than the standard classless realms.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Level 3 Milestone">
    Begin questing in your starting zone. Once you hit level 3, pay close attention to your chat and UI for a class quest prompt. This will lead you to your **Class Trainer** in hubs like Goldshire or Brill.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Specialization at Level 10">
    Upon reaching level 10, open the talent interface (`N` by default) and select your specialization. This is where you decide if your **Primalist** will be a Mountain King tank or a Wildwalker DPS.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gear Optimization">
    Look for "Bloodforged" items and RPG-stat gear in the open world. Unlike standard gear, these items often provide bonuses to unique class mechanics, such as increased Life Force generation or reduced Felfury decay.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Trainer Importance">
In Conquest of Azeroth, you do not automatically learn all spells. You must visit your class trainer regularly to purchase new ranks and utility spells. Skipping these visits will significantly slow down your leveling speed.
</Callout>

As you progress through the level 45, 55, and 58 milestones, the difficulty of open-world mobs increases. This is the time to refine your rotation. Use the **Manastorm** or early dungeons like Deadmines and Wailing Caverns to test your build's synergy with other players. By level 60, you should have a clear understanding of your class's crowd-control identity and resource management.

</Section>

<Callout type="tip" title="High Risk PvP">
If you choose the **High Risk PvP** phase, be aware that you can drop equipped gear upon death. However, this mode is the only way to farm certain Bloodforged upgrades in the open world. Only enter this phase if you are confident in your class's defensive tools.
</Callout>

The PvP system in Conquest of Azeroth emphasizes "Class Crowd-Control Identity." Instead of every class having a generic stun or fear, control tools are thematic. A **Chronomancer** excels at displacement and slows, while a **Witch Hunter** uses silver shots and hounds to lock down casters. This creates a more readable and strategic PvP environment compared to standard WoW expansions.
