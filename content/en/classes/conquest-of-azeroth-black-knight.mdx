<Section icon={Target} title="Black Knight Tanking Rotation">

The Black Knight rotation is divided into a pull phase, a mitigation maintenance loop, and an emergency recovery phase [1]. Because you lack a shield, establishing immediate threat and active shields is your top priority when engaging mobs [1].

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Pull & Gather">
    Initiate combat by casting **March of the Black King** to start generating rage [1]. Use **Chains of Darkness** to group up ranged and melee enemies directly on top of you, instantly securing solid AoE threat [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Apply Debuffs">
    Apply **Night Seal** to all targets to curse them and gain the **Dark Oath** buff, which reduces your overall damage taken [1]. Use **Saber Slash** to spread **Shadow Brand** across your primary targets [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Active Mitigation Loop">
    Once you parry an attack, immediately fire off **Noctis Blade** to activate your physical shield [1]. Weave in **Dawn Blade** to keep your mana topped off and maintain high single-target threat [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Desecrate Heal">
    When your health drops below 70%, consume your active Shadow Brands and Dark Oath stacks using **Desecrate** [1]. This instantly restores a massive portion of your health and mana pool [1].
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Survival Tip">
Always save your **Dark Tonic** (which acts like a Last Stand health boost) for when your healer is crowd-controlled or when facing high-burst boss mechanics [1].
</Callout>

</Section>

### Stat Priority for Gear Scaling
1. **Agility**: Your absolute best stat. It increases your armor, critical strike chance, and directly boosts your parry rating [1].
2. **Stamina**: Essential to survive the initial hits before your life steal and parry shields kick in [1].
3. **Shadow/Holy Damage**: Increases the healing output of your life-steal abilities like Witch Blight and Desecrate [1].
4. **Haste**: Lowers your global cooldowns, allowing you to react faster to parry procs [1].

<Callout type="info" title="Armor Limitations">
Remember that as a mail-wearing tank, your physical damage reduction from armor is lower than plate tanks. You must compensate by keeping your active shields and parry buffs up 100% of the time [1].
</Callout>
