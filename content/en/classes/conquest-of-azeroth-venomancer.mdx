<Section icon={Shield} title="Specialization Breakdown and Role Fit">

The four specializations split the class into clear jobs. That is the biggest reason the Venomancer feels so different from a standard hybrid. You are not just choosing a damage flavor; you are choosing how the class interacts with armor, healing, range, and battlefield control.

| Spec | Best Role | Combat Feel | Key Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fortitude | Tank | Slow, heavy, resilient | Damage reduction and group protection |
| Stalking | Melee DPS | Fast, aggressive, leech-heavy | Strong sustained pressure |
| Rot Weaver | Ranged DPS | Methodical, spell-focused | Poison crits and haste synergy |
| Vizir | Healer | Reactive, supportive, technical | HoTs, shields, and mana recovery |

Fortitude leans into indomitable scorpid forms and scales its toughness from intellect and agility. Stalking favors vicious predator forms and keeps enemies bleeding through close-range pressure. Rot Weaver spreads decay with spell haste and critical DoTs. Vizir converts the poison theme into restoration and channels support through Shadra’s Vigil.

<Callout type="warning" title="Pick One Main Job First">
Trying to build for tanking, melee pressure, caster pressure, and healing at the same time usually weakens the class. Start with one role, then expand later.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Utility, Control, and Rotation Priorities">

The class stands out because its utility is not decorative. Control, escape tools, and battlefield denial all support the core poison fantasy. That means your rotation should not be built only around damage buttons. It should also preserve safety, positioning, and uptime.

| Tool | Best Use | Role Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Cocoons | Lock down dangerous targets | Excellent for control-heavy pulls |
| Polymorph-style control | Reduce incoming pressure | Gives the group breathing room |
| Burrow | Reposition or avoid danger | Strong for survival and resets |
| Venom bolts | Spread pressure at range | Core Rot Weaver damage pattern |
| Serpent coils | Add poison-based damage flow | Helps maintain sustained DoT pressure |
| Toxic mushrooms | Control clustered enemies | Strong area denial in fights |
| Shadra’s Vigil | Heal, shield, and restore mana | Signature Vizir support tool |

A good Venomancer player uses control to create time, not just space. Every second an enemy is locked down, slowed, or redirected is another second where poison effects can do their work. That is especially true in harder dungeon pulls where the class's defensive tools and crowd control keep your healer or tank from being overwhelmed.

| Priority | Fortitude | Stalking | Rot Weaver | Vizir |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary job | Absorb damage | Sustain melee pressure | Maintain poison casts | Keep allies alive |
| Damage pattern | Low burst, steady threat | Close-range DoTs | Crit-enabled DoTs | Healing over time |
| Resource focus | Durability | Leech and uptime | Haste and crit | Haste and mana flow |

<Callout type="tip" title="Rotation Tip">
Keep your utility active as part of the rotation. Burrow, control effects, and poison setup are not panic buttons; they are part of your combat efficiency.
</Callout>

</Section>
