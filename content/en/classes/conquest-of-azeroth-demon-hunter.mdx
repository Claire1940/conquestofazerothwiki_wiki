<Section icon={Target} title="Core Mechanics and Rotation Strategy">

To play the **conquest of azeroth demon hunter** effectively, you must understand the interplay between your three main resources: Felfury Charges, Runes, and Rage. Unlike other classes that might rely on simple cooldowns, the Felsworn requires a tactical mindset to maintain high damage output or survivability.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Generate Felfury">
    Start your rotation with basic martial strikes or Fel spells to generate Felfury Charges. These are passive charges that regenerate over time but are consumed by your core combo abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Execute Combo Strikes">
    Follow specific ability sequences (e.g., Strike A -> Strike B) to generate a **Rune of Fel Fury**. Each rune generated empowers your next finisher, such as cleaving multiple foes or leaching health.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Demon Rage">
    As you fight, you will accumulate Rage. Once your Rage bar is maximized, you will automatically (or manually, depending on talents) transform into your **Demon Form**, significantly increasing your damage and unlocking new visuals.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Trigger Felstrikes">
    Keep your weapons infused. Many Felsworn attacks have a chance to trigger an additional Felstrike, dealing a burst of Fel flame to nearby enemies.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Mechanic | Effect | Resource Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Felstrikes** | Bonus AoE Fire Damage | Proc-based |
| **Runes of Fel Fury** | Empowers finishers/cleaves | Combo-generated |
| **Demon Form** | Massive Stat Boost | Rage-dependent |

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Don't spend all your Felfury Charges at once. If you run out of charges before generating enough Rage for Demon Form, your damage will drop significantly during the "recharge" window.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Callout type="tip" title="High Risk Gearing">
